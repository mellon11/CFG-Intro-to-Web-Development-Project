The climate change movement is a global social and academic movement focussed on pressuring governments and industry leaders to take action to reduce the affects of climate change. The movement is comprised of a rapidly growing and diverse coalition of individuals and groups alike, passionate about inspiring changes to our behaviours and attitudes towards our planet.

Here are just a few examples of the upcoming and influential voices in today's Climate Change discussion.

Dr Ayana Johnson

Dr Anyana Johnson is policy expert, conservation strategist and marine biologist working hard to emphasise the important connection between our oceans and climate change. She is the founder of Urban Ocean Lab (UOL), which strives to develop and analyse affective strategy to improve ecological health, use of ocean resources and climate change. Dr Ayana Johnson is also the co-editor of All We Can Save (a climate anthology) and the co-host of the popular podcast, How to Save a Planet.

Jamie Margolin

Jamie is the founder of youth climate organization, Zero Hour (an intersectional movement of youth climate activists). As the Latina, Jewish daughter of a Colombian immigrant, the Seattle native gave a TED talk about how climate change can be solved by addressing social injustices. Author of Youth to Power, Jamie aims to inspire and guide other young people to take action and create lasting changes in our attitudes and actions towards climate change.
